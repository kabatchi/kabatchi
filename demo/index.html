Biography

Kabatchi's Biography

Kabatchi was born on 31st August 1995 at Likuni Hospital in Lilongwe going to various primary schools and secondary schools now studying computer
I started doing music long ago in terms of composing and writing songs but it was in 2015 i recorded my first song. And i have released couple of singles and I'm planning to drop Extended Playlist (EP) in 2022. The one who inspired me to become a musician is my farther, he loves music and playing guitar and whenever when I'm with him is music. Despite giving people good music but my aim is to promote music in all bases, and i will introduce a recording rebel to sign artists there are a lot of talent out there lacking potential to expose it. In Malawian music industry things are improving in all bases especially our production you can agree with me people have been doing good songs but of low quality and when it comes to support people they have come to realisation that working with artists it help in different ways. My biggest challenge is financial and it's a challenge if every artist who just venture into music industry, i made sure to work and hustle hard as do i do it myself without a manager and I'm not in any music duo as well. In 4 years i see Kabatchi as someone growing in music industry by giving people good music and helping other talented artist as i said we have talented youths in ghettos lacking exposure. When I recorded my first song people was wow even the producer himself that time Ashavwint was like no Kabatchi you have been doing this don't lie. Music changes me in different ways Like you must be a hard worker everything to work for you and be humble people hate arrogant everywhere and i have learnt to be strong when something get tough don't give up. Apart from being a talent hunter i enjoy recording sessions that thing teach me a lot, I do Reggae Dancehall music because i like the vibe reggae music never die. Every time I feel like giving up i remember there's Jehovah who never sleeps on us and his only son Jesus who is always there for for us. And my advice to my fellow musicians is be humble, work hard and learn to invest money we earn for our future.
